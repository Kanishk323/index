How to Use in Industry

While our model is for a science fair, the core principles can be scaled up dramatically for industrial use. This advanced method is known as High-Temperature Steam Electrolysis.

Advanced Concentrators

  • Industrial plants use large fields of mirrors (heliostats) to focus sunlight on a single tower.
  • Automated tracking systems ensure mirrors are always perfectly aligned with the sun for maximum efficiency.

High-Temperature Electrolysis

  • Industrial production uses Solid Oxide Electrolysis Cells (SOECs), which are specifically designed for this process.
  • This technology uses heat (from steam) to reduce the electrical energy required for splitting water, resulting in higher overall efficiency.

Waste Heat Integration

  • Excess heat from other industrial processes can be captured and used to create the steam.
  • This reduces the need for primary energy input, making the overall system extremely efficient.

How the System Works

  • The Mirrors: These mirrors are designed to reflect and concentrate the sun's rays onto a single point, generating intense heat.
  • The Boiler: The concentrated heat is directed at a boiler, which rapidly turns water into high-temperature steam.
  • The Turbine/Motor: The powerful steam is used to spin a turbine, which in turn drives a motor to generate electricity.
  • The Electrolyzer: This electricity is then fed into an electrolyzer to split the water molecules ($H_2O$) into hydrogen ($H_2$) and oxygen ($O_2$).
